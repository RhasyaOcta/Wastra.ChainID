Our Research

WASTRA-CHAIN ID

Bogor Batik is a valuable cultural heritage, still crafted using traditional techniques such as the canting. However, in the global market, it faces a major challenge: declining trust in authenticity due to counterfeit products and weak traceability systems.

Today's international consumers demand more than just products—they expect transparency in production processes, material authenticity, and environmental impact. Without innovation, Bogor Batik risks being overshadowed by mass-produced imitations.

To address this issue, WASTRA-CHAIN ID offers a blockchain-based certification system that assigns a secure digital identity to each piece of batik, recording its entire production history.

The Challenge

The Problem & Our Solution

The Authenticity Crisis

Bogor Batik artisans face a critical market challenge: weak systems for verifying authenticity. International buyers actively avoid "gray-market" products with uncertain origins, and traditional paper-based certifications are easily forged.

Without robust traceability, Bogor Batik is often mistaken for factory-made printed textiles, causing market value to plummet and threatening centuries of cultural heritage.

Blockchain-Powered Authenticity

WASTRA-CHAIN ID uses blockchain technology to create immutable, unalterable digital records of every batik piece. By scanning a QR code, overseas buyers see a complete digital "trace" proving authenticity, artisan identity, materials used, and production timeline.

Batik Kujang

The Kujang motif is the most iconic symbol of Bogor Batik, inspired by the traditional Sundanese weapon that represents strength, protection, and cultural pride. Each stroke preserves centuries of heritage, hand-drawn using the canting technique passed down through generations of artisans.

Signature Motif
02

Batik Katulampa

Named after the iconic Katulampa waterfall in Bogor, this motif captures the region's natural beauty and its powerful connection to water. The flowing patterns symbolise resilience, purity, and the constant flow of tradition alongside modern progress.

Batik Kijang Papasangan

The Kijang (Deer) Papasangan motif embodies harmony and grace, representing the elegant deer that inhabits Bogor's forests. This design symbolises balance between nature and culture, depicting a pair in perfect union—a metaphor for the complementary relationship between tradition and progress.
